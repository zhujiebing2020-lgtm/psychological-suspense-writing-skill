# Psychological Suspense Writing

## Purpose

Use this skill to build and refine psychological suspense fiction where mystery, intimacy, bodily evidence, and moral ambiguity interlock.

This is not a generic thriller template. It is for stories where clues are also relationship pressure points, violence is psychological and aesthetic rather than merely graphic, and the investigation gradually reveals how people possess, preserve, misread, or consume one another.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the task: concept, outline, chapter draft, expansion, revision, review, publication cleanup, or longform planning.
2. Inspect any existing outline, chapters, character notes, and clue lists available in the workspace.
3. Decide the current unit's load:
   - one suspense movement
   - one relationship scene
   - one body/mental pressure node
   - one viewpoint texture
   - one clean hook or delayed return
4. Check whether the scene advances at least two of three lines:
   - suspense line: evidence, access, timeline, procedure, reveal
   - emotional line: desire, control, betrayal, dependency, avoidance
   - theme line: possession, preservation, guilt, memory, identity, body
5. Draft or revise with concrete scenes, objects, gestures, and procedure. Do not pad with explanation.
6. Run the relevant checklist from `references/revision-checklist.md`.

## Chapter Review Stance

When reviewing a chapter, prioritize:

- whether it stands as a complete reading unit
- whether it advances at least two of suspense, emotion, theme
- whether evidence sources are plausible
- whether the protagonist investigates from their specific wound, not generic curiosity
- whether the absent/dead/missing character returns as a person, not only a plot object
- whether antagonistic forces remain human, specific, and not overexplained
- whether the hook opens one strong question rather than stacking several discoveries

## Style Rules

- Prefer concrete sensory pressure over abstract theme explanation.
- Let objects, timing, body posture, procedural details, and withheld speech carry meaning.
- Avoid organ-list plotting, clue dumping, and exposition masquerading as dialogue.
- Keep violence aesthetic rooted in bodily stimulus plus precise psychological mechanism, not gore alone.
- Do not announce moral equivalences too early. Let readers discover them through repeated scenes.

## Output Habits

When writing chapters, keep separate chapter files if the project uses separate files.

When creating reusable project-specific material, keep it separate from this general skill. This skill should remain portable across stories.
